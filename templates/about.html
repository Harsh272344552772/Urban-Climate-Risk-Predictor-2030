About Urban Climate Risk Predictor

The Urban Climate Risk Predictor is a sophisticated tool designed to help cities, urban planners, and communities understand and prepare for climate-related risks through 2030. Our predictive models combine historical climate data, urban infrastructure assessment, and population trends to provide actionable insights.

Our Methodology

Data Collection

We gather data from multiple sources including weather stations, satellite imagery, urban infrastructure databases, and population censuses.

Predictive Modeling

Our algorithms use machine learning techniques to identify patterns and predict future climate risks based on historical trends and projections.

Risk Assessment

We evaluate vulnerabilities in urban areas by analyzing infrastructure resilience, population density, and adaptive capacity.

Technologies Used

  • Python - Core data processing and machine learning
  • NumPy & Pandas - Data manipulation and analysis
  • Matplotlib - Visualization of climate data
  • Flask - Web application framework
  • SQLAlchemy - Database ORM
  • JavaScript - Interactive visualizations

Our Team

Our interdisciplinary team consists of climate scientists, urban planners, data analysts, and software engineers committed to helping cities become more resilient to climate change.
